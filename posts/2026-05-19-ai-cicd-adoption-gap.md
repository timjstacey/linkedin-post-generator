90% of developers use AI tools at work every day. 73% of their organizations have zero AI inside CI/CD pipelines.

JetBrains published this gap in April, drawing from three surveys: AI Pulse (January 2026), State of Developer Ecosystem, and State of CI/CD Tools. The AI Pulse survey put the abstention rate higher, at 78.2%.

An IDE suggestion costs nothing to reject. Your pipeline result determines what ships. Teams need reproducible, auditable signals there, and 60% of organizations holding back on pipeline AI cite unclear use cases. Thirty-six percent cite lack of trust in AI results. Thirty-three percent cite data privacy.

When AI enters CI/CD, teams use it for failure diagnosis first. TeamCity 2026.1, released this month, added MCP server support so AI agents can parse failing builds and surface probable root causes without leaving the terminal. CloudBees Smart Tests takes a narrower approach: rather than diagnosing failures, it skips irrelevant tests. Their production benchmarks put roughly a third of CI failures as flaky, triggered by infrastructure noise rather than code changes. AI-driven test selection analyzes the diff and runs only the tests tied to that change.

Reducing noise is how AI earns pipeline trust.

Every commit you push today runs AI-generated code through your pipeline. Whether AI helps decide which tests to run is the next gap to close.
